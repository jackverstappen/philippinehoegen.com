A performative presentation during DIS_SEMINAR / Kunst en Onderzoek (Art and Research), titled Ventriloquists III: a ±25-minute performance which is both a research method in, and a presentation of, the practice-based research Hoegen is conducting at CARADT entitled 'The Self as a Relational Infrastructure in Process'. This is a practice-based enquiry into the production of different versions of ourselves, the technologies and processes used to produce these versions, and how to understand the self within the existence of these multiple versions.

In the research Hoegen approaches and activates performance explicitly as a research strategy: a way of thinking that involves the physical. The question posed and discussed during the seminar was: what happens to the sharability and digestibility of a complex discourse on subjectivity and personhood when researched/presented through performance? And what is the (feedback) effect of this form on the content of the research?
